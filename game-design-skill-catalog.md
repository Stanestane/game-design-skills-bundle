# Game Design Skill Catalog

A quick index of the game design skills currently available in the workspace.

## Emotional / thematic direction

### game-design-emotional-canvas
Define the emotional core, atmosphere, and vibe of a game, feature, event, or theme. Best for emotional identity, moodboards, and tone alignment.

## General decision framework

### game-design-grow-design
Use the GROW model to structure feature discussions through Goal, Reality, Options, and Will. Good for teams that need more process and clearer decisions.

## Motivation / psychology audits

### game-design-player-motivation-audit
Audit what kinds of motivation a design creates: reward-driven, status-driven, value-endorsed, identity-based, or intrinsic.

### game-design-player-need-satisfaction-audit
Audit whether a design satisfies or denies autonomy, competence, and relatedness. Good for spotting emotionally nourishing versus quietly depleting systems.

## Prototyping / uncertainty

### game-design-unknown-unknowns-prototyping
Map uncertainty, identify hidden unknowns, and decide what should be prototyped before production commitment.

## GROW-derived workflow family

### game-design-goal-framing
Turn a vague idea into a clear feature goal with purpose, fit, success criteria, and constraints.

### game-design-option-generation
Generate multiple credible solution paths before committing to one.

### game-design-five-options
Force explicit comparison across at least five candidate approaches.

### game-design-roadblock-reframing
Get unstuck by isolating the main blocker, imagining it removed, and deriving workaround paths.

### game-design-ideal-outcome-backcasting
Start from the ideal player-facing result and work backward to the design steps needed to reach it.

### game-design-transformative-reuse
Solve new problems by adapting, extending, recombining, or retuning what already exists.

### game-design-feature-prioritization
Compare candidate options by impact, cost, risk, fit, and timing to decide what to do now versus later.

## FTUE / onboarding audits

### game-design-ftue-hero-journey-audit
Audit FTUE and onboarding flows through the Hero's Journey lens: call to adventure, mentor, threshold crossing, early autonomy, and first-session emotional connection.

## Pitch / publishing audits

### game-design-pitch-deck-audit
Audit a game pitch deck for structure, clarity, dream-versus-proof balance, business-case strength, readability, and publisher-fit.

## Creative disruption / unblockers

### game-design-creative-unblocker
Use oblique prompts, sideways reframing, and playful provocations to break creative block and generate weird-but-useful next directions.

## Adversarial / red-team audits

### design-red-team-audit
Perform a hostile-but-constructive pre-mortem on a feature, system, pitch, or roadmap idea by assuming failure and identifying the most credible ways it breaks.

## Suggested ways to combine them

- **Need clarity first** -> `game-design-goal-framing`
- **Need more solution paths** -> `game-design-option-generation` or `game-design-five-options`
- **Need to get unstuck** -> `game-design-roadblock-reframing` or `game-design-creative-unblocker`
- **Need stronger emotional direction** -> `game-design-emotional-canvas`
- **Need better psychological diagnosis** -> `game-design-player-motivation-audit` or `game-design-player-need-satisfaction-audit`
- **Need to decide what to prototype** -> `game-design-unknown-unknowns-prototyping`
- **Need to pick what to do now** -> `game-design-feature-prioritization`

## Notes

- The GROW-derived skills are mostly workflow skills.
- The motivation and need-satisfaction skills are mostly audit skills.
- `game-design-creative-unblocker` is intentionally sillier and more disruptive than the rest of the pack.
